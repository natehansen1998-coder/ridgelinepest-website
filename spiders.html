Dealing with a Spider Problem?

Whether you've spotted a few webs in the corners or you're finding spiders throughout your home, you're not alone. Utah County's climate makes it a perfect habitat for many spider species—including some that can be dangerous.

At Ridgeline Pest Control, we specialize in comprehensive spider control. We don't just knock down webs; we eliminate spiders at their source, treat the areas where they hide, and create barriers to keep them from returning.

Common Spiders in Utah County

Understanding which spiders you're dealing with helps us provide the most effective treatment. Here are the most common species we encounter:

Black Widows

Shiny black with red hourglass marking. Venomous bite requires medical attention. Found in dark, undisturbed areas.

Hobo Spiders

Brown with chevron pattern. Fast runners. Common in basements, garages, and ground-level areas.

Wolf Spiders

Large, hairy, brown spiders. They don't spin webs but actively hunt. Often found outdoors but enter homes.

House Spiders

Small, brown spiders that create messy webs in corners. Harmless but unsightly and persistent.

⚠ Black Widow Safety Warning

Black widow spiders are present in Utah County and their bites can be medically significant, especially for children, elderly, and pets. If you suspect black widows on your property, avoid disturbing them and call us immediately. Common hiding spots include woodpiles, outdoor storage, garages, and dark corners.

Why Do I Have So Many Spiders?

Spiders follow their food. If your home has a lot of spiders, it usually means you have a lot of other insects that spiders feed on. Our comprehensive pest control approach addresses this by:

  • Reducing prey insects - Eliminating the bugs that attract spiders in the first place
  • Sealing entry points - Making it harder for both spiders and their prey to enter
  • Removing harborage areas - Eliminating conditions that favor spider populations
  • Creating barriers - Residual treatments that kill spiders on contact

What to Expect After Treatment

After our spider control service, here's what you can expect:

  • Immediate - All visible webs removed and surfaces treated
  • First Week - You may see some spider activity as they contact treated areas
  • Weeks 2-4 - Significant reduction in spider sightings
  • Ongoing - Our barrier treatments continue working for weeks, killing spiders that try to enter

Preventing Spider Problems

While our treatments provide excellent protection, these tips can help reduce spider activity around your home:

  • Keep outdoor lights off or use yellow "bug lights" that attract fewer insects
  • Remove clutter in garages, basements, and storage areas
  • Seal cracks around windows, doors, and foundations
  • Keep vegetation trimmed away from the house
  • Store firewood away from the home and inspect before bringing inside
  • Reduce moisture in basements and crawl spaces
  • Install door sweeps and repair torn screens
