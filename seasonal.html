Why Pest Problems Change With the Seasons

Utah County experiences dramatic seasonal changes, and so do our pest problems. From the warm valleys to the mountain foothills, temperature shifts, moisture levels, and food availability all influence which pests become active and when they'll try to invade your home.

Understanding these seasonal patterns is key to effective pest control. At Ridgeline Pest Control, we adjust our treatment strategies throughout the year to target the specific pests that are most active in each season, giving you continuous protection.

🌸 Spring Pest Control (March - May)

As Utah's temperatures warm up, dormant pests wake up hungry and ready to reproduce. Spring is when many pest problems begin, making early intervention crucial.

Common Spring Pests

Ants

Colonies emerge seeking food and water after winter dormancy

Spiders

Become active and start building webs around homes

Termites

Swarmers emerge to establish new colonies

Wasps

Queens begin building new nests

Why They're Active

Warming soil temperatures trigger ant colonies to expand and forage. Melting snow creates moisture that attracts many pests. Termite swarms typically occur on warm days following rain. Overwintering spider eggs begin hatching.

Our Spring Treatment Approach

  • Perimeter barrier treatments to stop ants before they enter
  • Foundation sprays targeting emerging spiders
  • Termite inspections to catch swarmers early
  • Wasp nest removal before colonies grow large
  • De-webbing of exterior surfaces

Spring Prevention Tips:

  • Seal cracks in your foundation before ants find them
  • Clean gutters to eliminate moisture buildup
  • Store firewood away from the house
  • Trim vegetation touching exterior walls
  • Schedule a termite inspection if you haven't had one

☀ Summer Pest Control (June - August)

Summer is peak pest season in Utah County. Hot temperatures and outdoor activities mean maximum pest exposure. This is when infestations can grow rapidly if left unchecked.

Common Summer Pests

Wasps & Hornets

Colonies at peak size, aggressive near food

Mosquitoes

Breeding in standing water, active at dawn/dusk

Ants

Maximum foraging activity, multiple colonies

Earwigs

Seeking cool, moist areas in homes

Why They're Active

High temperatures accelerate insect metabolism and reproduction. Outdoor cooking and gatherings attract stinging insects. Drought conditions drive pests indoors seeking water. Long days mean more hours of pest activity.

Our Summer Treatment Approach

  • Aggressive ant colony elimination treatments
  • Wasp and hornet nest removal services
  • Exterior barrier applications with residual products
  • Targeted treatments for earwigs and other moisture pests
  • Advice on reducing mosquito breeding sites

Summer Prevention Tips:

  • Eliminate standing water where mosquitoes breed
  • Keep food covered during outdoor events
  • Repair window and door screens
  • Don't leave pet food outside overnight
  • Address wasp nests while they're still small

🍂 Fall Pest Control (September - November)

As temperatures drop, pests start looking for warm places to overwinter—and your home is the perfect target. Fall is critical for preventing winter infestations.

Common Fall Pests

Rodents

Mice and rats seeking shelter before winter

Spiders

Moving indoors for warmth and prey

Box Elder Bugs

Congregating on sunny walls, entering homes

Stink Bugs

Seeking overwintering sites in wall voids

Why They're Active

Cooling temperatures trigger survival instincts. Rodents can sense approaching cold and begin seeking indoor harborage. Box elder bugs swarm on south-facing walls to absorb heat. Spiders follow their prey insects indoors.

Our Fall Treatment Approach

  • Rodent exclusion services to seal entry points
  • Exterior treatments targeting overwintering pests
  • Spider control inside and outside
  • Box elder bug and stink bug barrier treatments
  • Inspection of attics and basements for rodent activity

Fall Prevention Tips:

  • Seal gaps around pipes, wires, and utilities
  • Install door sweeps on all exterior doors
  • Screen attic and foundation vents
  • Store seasonal items in sealed containers
  • Clean up fallen fruit and garden debris

❄ Winter Pest Control (December - February)

Winter doesn't mean pest problems disappear—they just move indoors. Rodents become the primary concern, and overwintering insects can emerge on warm days.

Common Winter Pests

Mice

Active year-round, breeding in walls and attics

Rats

Seeking food sources in homes and garages

Overwintering Insects

Emerging from walls on warm days

Spiders

Active in basements and crawl spaces

Why They're Active

Mice and rats don't hibernate—they remain active year-round inside your home. They can reproduce every 3 weeks, meaning a small problem can become a major infestation. Overwintering insects in wall voids may emerge during warm spells or when heating activates them.

Our Winter Treatment Approach

  • Rodent trapping and removal programs
  • Exclusion repairs to prevent future entry
  • Monitoring stations to detect activity early
  • Treatment of overwintering pest harborage areas
  • Attic and crawl space inspections

Winter Prevention Tips:

  • Store food in sealed, rodent-proof containers
  • Keep storage areas clean and organized
  • Don't leave garage doors open
  • Listen for scratching sounds in walls at night
  • Check for droppings in cabinets and pantries
