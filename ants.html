Common Ants in Utah County

Utah is home to several ant species that commonly invade homes and businesses. Proper identification is key to effective treatment:

Pavement Ants

Small, dark brown ants that nest in cracks in pavement and foundations. Common kitchen invaders.

Carpenter Ants

Large black ants that can damage wood structures. They don't eat wood but excavate it for nesting.

Sugar Ants

Small ants attracted to sweets and moisture. Often found near sinks, counters, and food sources.

Harvester Ants

Red to brown ants found in yards. Known for painful stings and creating large bare spots in lawns.

⚠ Carpenter Ant Warning

Carpenter ants can cause significant structural damage to your home over time. If you're seeing large black ants, especially near wood structures or hearing rustling sounds in walls, contact us immediately for an inspection.

  • Days 3-7 - Ant activity will significantly decrease as the colony is affected by the bait.
  • Week 2 - Most ant activity should be eliminated. The barrier treatment will continue protecting your home.
  • Ongoing - Our treatments provide residual protection for weeks. We back it up with our satisfaction guarantee.

Preventing Future Ant Problems

While our treatments provide excellent protection, here are some tips to help prevent ant problems:

Ant Prevention Tips:

  • Keep kitchen surfaces clean and free of crumbs
  • Store food in sealed containers
  • Fix leaky pipes and faucets (ants need water)
  • Seal cracks and gaps around windows, doors, and foundations
  • Keep tree branches and shrubs trimmed away from the house
  • Remove wood debris and mulch from touching the foundation
  • Address moisture issues in basements and crawl spaces
