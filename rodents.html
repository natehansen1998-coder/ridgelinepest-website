Signs You Have a Rodent Problem

Rodents are secretive creatures, but they leave behind telltale signs. If you've noticed any of the following, you likely have mice or rats in your home:

  • Droppings - Small, dark pellets found along walls, in cabinets, or near food sources
  • Gnaw marks - Fresh gnawing on food packaging, wood, or plastic
  • Scratching sounds - Noises in walls or ceilings, especially at night
  • Nests - Shredded paper, fabric, or insulation in hidden areas
  • Rub marks - Dark smudges along walls from oily fur
  • Actual sightings - Seeing a mouse or rat, especially during daylight, indicates a significant infestation

⚠ Health Risks of Rodent Infestations

Rodents pose serious health risks to your family. They can spread diseases including Hantavirus, Salmonella, and Leptospirosis. Their droppings, urine, and dander can trigger allergies and asthma. Additionally, rodents cause property damage by gnawing on wires (fire hazard), insulation, and structural materials.

Rodents We Control

Utah County is home to several rodent species that commonly invade homes and businesses:

House Mice

Small, gray-brown mice that are the most common home invaders. Excellent climbers that can squeeze through tiny gaps.

Deer Mice

Brown mice with white undersides. Common in rural and suburban areas. Carriers of Hantavirus.

Norway Rats

Large, brown rats that burrow near foundations. Found in basements, sewers, and ground-level areas.

Roof Rats

Sleek, dark rats excellent at climbing. Found in attics, trees, and upper levels of buildings.

Why Rodent Problems Get Worse Without Action

Rodents reproduce rapidly. A single pair of mice can produce up to 60 offspring in a year, and those offspring can begin reproducing within weeks. What starts as a minor problem can become a major infestation very quickly. Early intervention is crucial.

Preventing Rodent Problems

These tips can help make your home less attractive to rodents:

  • Store food in airtight containers
  • Keep pet food in sealed containers and don't leave it out overnight
  • Clean up crumbs and spills immediately
  • Take out garbage regularly and use secure trash cans
  • Eliminate water sources (fix leaky pipes, don't leave water sitting)
  • Declutter storage areas to reduce nesting sites
  • Store firewood away from the house
  • Keep grass short and vegetation trimmed near the foundation
